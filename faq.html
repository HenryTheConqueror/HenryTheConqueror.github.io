FAQ

1. Printing Documents from Your Laptop at HZ

To print documents from your laptop at HZ, you need to obtain a HZ facility card. This card grants access to printing services, coffee/tea machines, and parking. To acquire your facility card, you must upload a photo of yourself. Detailed instructions on how to do this are provided in the Staff Introduction document.

2. Scanning Documents and Sending Them to Your Laptop

While specific instructions for scanning documents are not detailed, HZ provides approximately 400 computers available for students across various locations, including the library, classrooms, and course-related areas such as laboratories and simulation rooms. These facilities are equipped to support student needs.

3. Purchasing Items on the HZ Webshop

Tickets for HZ Cult's programme can be purchased through their webshop at www.hzcult.nl. This platform is used for various events and activities organized by HZ.

4. Registering for Courses and Exams at HZ

Students must register for courses via OSIRIS Student. Registration for courses is typically handled by the program for the first two test occasions in the first year. For subsequent courses, students are responsible for their own registration. It's important to register before the deadline, which is usually one week before the start of the course.

5. Parking Your Car at HZ

Middelburg (Het Groene Woud): It is not possible to park directly at the HZ. There is one special parking space available on the grounds for people with disabilities and/or chronic conditions. The car park on Kousteensedijk has a number of spaces for visitors, staff, and students. Upon showing your entry card, you can obtain an exit card at the JRCZ service desk.

Vlissingen (Edisonweg): There are parking areas around the campus. If parking is full, you can also park at Orionis or Scalda. The NS train station is a 10-minute walk away.

6. HZ Institutional Plan 2022-2027

The HZ Institutional Plan 2022-2027 outlines the university's mission, vision, ambitions, and core values. It emphasizes the commitment to contribute to a better world through education and research. The plan is a result of close collaboration with internal and external stakeholders.

7. Mission and Vision at HZ

HZ's mission and vision are articulated in the HZ Institutional Plan 2022-2027. The university aims to be a valued institute in areas such as water, energy, and vitality, and to be a partner for social missions in the region. These ambitions guide the way HZ organizes its education and research.

8. Acceptable Use Policies for ICT Resources

HZ has established Acceptable Use Policies for both students and employees to ensure responsible and secure use of ICT resources. These policies cover aspects such as system and network security, prevention of misuse, protection of personal data, and guidelines for the use of social media. They aim to balance the need for effective ICT use with the privacy and rights of individuals.

9. Focus of the ICT Program at HZ

The Information and Communication Technology (ICT) program at HZ focuses on solving problems and improving processes using ICT. It emphasizes analytical and problem-solving skills, with a strong foundation in programming. The program includes real-life cases and covers areas such as data science, software engineering, and IT consulting, preparing students for professional roles in the ICT field.

10. Entry Requirements for the ICT Program

For the ICT program at HZ, there are no extra entry requirements beyond the general admission criteria. Applicants need to demonstrate proficiency in English, equivalent to an IELTS score of 6.0. Specific subject requirements may vary, and it's advisable to check the detailed admission criteria for the most accurate information.
